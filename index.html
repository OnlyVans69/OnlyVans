Van Knowledge: How to Identify Parts for Your Dodge B300

Vintage Dodge vans like the B100, B200, and B300 are known for their unique mix of utility and character. But when you're searching for replacement parts—especially interior switches, speedometers, or side glass—knowing your exact model and generation is crucial.

1. Start with the VIN

The VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) on your B300 holds the key. Look for it on the dashboard near the windshield or inside the driver-side door frame. The 5th digit often identifies engine type, and the 10th digit reveals the model year.

2. Know the Generations

  • 1971–1977: First-gen boxier vans with round headlights
  • 1978–1993: Second-gen, more aerodynamic nose, square lamps
  • 1994–2003: Modernized Ram Van era

This helps narrow down what parts fit—especially for dashboards, wiring harnesses, and trim.

3. Compare Part Numbers

OEM part numbers (often stamped or labeled on the item) are your best friend. At OnlyVans, we list known part numbers in each eBay listing to help match your exact needs.

Van Knowledge: What’s the Rarest Chevy G-Series Van Part?

The Chevy G10, G20, and G30 vans were beloved workhorses — but today, some parts are nearly extinct.

Top 3 Rarest Parts:

Van Knowledge: How to Decode Ford Econoline Model Numbers

Not sure whether you have an E-150, E-250, or E-350? Here's how to decode your Econoline model:

Basic Breakdown:

  • E-150: 1/2-ton van — common for personal and light commercial use
  • E-250: 3/4-ton — heavier suspension and higher GVWR
  • E-350: 1-ton — heavy-duty axles and often dual rear wheels

Look for the emblem on the front fender, or decode the VIN 6th–7th digits to confirm your exact variant.

Knowing your model helps ensure you’re buying the correct brake parts, suspension, and wheels — especially across the 1980–2000 span.
